RSA Wills

This Will Template is used to make a Will for only liquid (cash) assets, such as bank accounts, investments, insurance policies (including Group Life Insurance put in place by employers), gratuity, Retirement Saving Accounts (RSA) with PFAs and debt owed to you by persons etc.

This Will Template cannot cover landed assets but is designed to aid persons who own only liquid assets in protecting same. Therefore, if you have landed assets to protect, you should opt for the Simple Will template.

However, it is imperative to know that this Will just like all Wills only comes into effect after the demise of the Testator (Maker).

RSA Trust

This Trust template is used to make a Trust for only liquid (cash) assets, such as bank accounts, investments, insurance policies (including Group Life Insurance put in place by employers), gratuity, Retirement Saving Accounts (RSA) with PFAs and debt owed to you by persons etc.

This Trust template cannot cover landed assets but is designed to aid persons who own only liquid assets in protecting same. Therefore, if you have landed assets to protect, you should opt for the Simple Will template.

Future obligations can be created for the Trustees over the assets in the Trust with respect to management and distribution of assets and you can also create obligations that begin to run immediately the Trust is created.

A Trust, unlike a Will, comes into effect while the Settlor (Maker) is alive.

Simple Will

A Simple Will is a vehicle that allows for easy and straight forward distribution of assets. A Simple Will does not manage assets.

However, a Simple Will can take all type of assets whether Landed assets, shares, bank accounts, investments, insurance policies (including Group Life Insurance put in place by employers), gratuity, Retirement Saving Accounts (RSA) with PFAs and debt owed to the Testator by other persons.

A Simple Will can be created on WillPower. If you would rather a more personal touch, please contact us to speak with one of our professionals who would prepare the Will in line with your wishes and instructions.

Islamic Will

If you are a practicing Muslim, you can specify such in WillPower. The application distributes properties to beneficiaries in a way compliant with the teachings of the Quran and the Sunnah.

Islamic Law allows a Muslim Testator to only make a Will that freely distributes up to 1/3rd of his/her entire estate, a Muslim Will cannot be used to reduce, alter or exclude legal heirs (blood relatives) whose share in the Testator’s Estate are already apportioned in the Quran or Sunnah. Succinctly explained, while the Testator of a Muslim Will can voluntary distribute up to 1/3rd of his entire Estate as he desires, the remaining 2/3rd must be left for his legal heirs and distributed in accordance with the distribution system laid down in Islam.

Comprehensive Will

A Comprehensive Will is a more elaborate Will that allows for every kind of situation and complexity attached to the Testator. Unlike the Simple Will which allows for a straight forward distribution of assets where the beneficiary situation or asset situation are well identifiable and/or ascertainable, the Comprehensive will can cover for uncertainties or certain conditions.

The Comprehensive Will can take all type of assets whether Landed assets, shares, bank accounts, investments, insurance policies (including Group Life Insurance put in place by employers), gratuity, Retirement Saving Accounts (RSA) with PFAs and debt owed to you by persons, you can even give instruction on the payment of debts you people from the money you have etc.

Comprehensive Trust

A Comprehensive Trust is a very elaborate Trust arrangement that can accommodate various obligations which the Trustee would be required to perform on behalf of the settlor.

Comprehensive Trust can accommodate the inclusion of Landed assets, shares (which would have to be transferred into the name of the Trust), alongside other movable assets. Obligations such management and payment of certain recurring fees, management of assets etc., can be provided for in a comprehensive Trust.
